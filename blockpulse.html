Redesigning a Blockchain-Based Fundraising Platform for SMEs and Investors

Overview

This case study outlines the redesign of a blockchain-powered fundraising platform aimed at helping SMEs raise capital and allowing investors to discover and fund high-potential businesses completely digitally. The redesign focused on improving brand awareness, onboarding, usability, and overall user experience to raise platform adoption and engagement.

Problem Definition

Despite a strong technological foundation and the growing relevance of blockchain in fundraising, the platform was struggling with:

  • Low product/brand awareness among potential SME clients and investors.
  • Low conversion rates on the marketing website (from visit to sign-up).
  • High user drop-off rates during onboarding and campaign creation.
  • Usability issues throughout the core product (e.g., fund-raising setup, investment flows).
  • Limited active users (MAUs) despite sign-ups, indicating poor retention and value delivery.

Objectives

Primary Goals

  • Increase Monthly Active Users (MAUs) by 40% in 6 months.
  • Improve conversion rate on the marketing site by 50%.
  • Improve usability and task success rate on key user flows (onboarding, investment, campaign creation).

Secondary Goals

  • Clarify brand positioning and messaging to attract high-quality leads. Why should investors and founders trust blockpulse?
  • Build trust in blockchain-backed transactions via better UX and communication.
  • Improve mobile accessibility for better reach in emerging markets.

Target Users

  • Small and Medium Enterprises (SMEs): Seeking non-traditional, faster capital raising methods.
  • Retail & Institutional Investors: Looking for vetted investment opportunities in early-stage and growth businesses, with secure and transparent transactions.

Research & Discovery

1. Stakeholder Interviews

Uncovered business goals and friction points such as:

  • Low confidence in user journey for both SMEs and investors.
  • Unclear value proposition and inconsistent messaging on the marketing site.

2. User Interviews & Surveys

Insights from 20 users (10 SMEs, 10 investors) revealed:

  • Confusion around blockchain benefits.
  • Overwhelming jargon and poor onboarding.
  • Lack of guidance in setting up fundraising campaigns or making first investments.

3. Heuristic Evaluation & UX Audit

Findings included:

  • Poor information hierarchy & architecture.
  • Broken navigation logic.
  • Lack of feedback on transactional actions.
  • Inconsistent UI components.

Design Strategy

Brand and Marketing Site Revamp

  • New Brand Identity: Created a visual and messaging system centered on trust, transparency, and growth.
  • Homepage Redesign: Clear value proposition, targeted CTAs for SMEs and investors.
  • Content Strategy: Educational content and interactive explainers on how blockchain enhances fundraising.

Platform UX Overhaul

Key user journeys improved:

a. SME Campaign Creation Flow

  • Introduced a wizard-style setup for campaigns.
  • Contextual tips, progress indicators, and smart defaults.
  • Real-time previews and success estimates.

b. Investor Discovery & Investment Flow

  • Added personalised recommendations, filters, and risk levels.
  • Streamlined wallet setup and KYC verification.
  • Transparent milestone-based funding visuals.

Mobile Optimisation

  • Responsive design overhaul for all core flows.
  • Introduced a lightweight progressive web app (PWA) to ensure accessibility without requiring a native download.

Onboarding & Help Systems

  • Contextual walkthroughs.
  • Embedded support via live chat and smart FAQs.
  • Gamified onboarding checklist (e.g., "Complete your first investment, earn bonus tokens").

Prototyping & Testing

  • Mid-fi and Hi-fi prototypes tested with 10 returning and 10 new users.
  • Conducted usability testing on:
    • Campaign creation.
    • Investor browsing/investment.
    • Wallet connection and DKYC.
  • Used heatmaps and session recordings on new site design.

Design System & Implementation

  • Introduced a modular design system (tokens, components, patterns) for scalability.
  • Used Figma for design, with Storybook for component documentation.
  • Collaborated with devs in weekly design QA loops to ensure pixel-perfect handoffs.

Lessons Learned

  • Clear educational UI is essential when introducing blockchain tech to non-tech-savvy users.
  • Trust signals (like third-party audits, testimonials, secure wallet integrations) directly influence conversion.
  • A modular onboarding strategy—tailored by user role—works better than a generic, one-size-fits-all approach.
  • Regular user testing post-launch was critical to rapid iteration and performance gains.
